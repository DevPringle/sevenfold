Sevenfold is no more.

Published

Sevenfold

Rather than let this site go to waste before scrapping it I thought I'd do a write up here instead of scattered messages in Discord.

TLDR - Sevenfold as you know it is shutting down.

However Sevenfold isn't actually what anyone thought it was. Not entirely at least. If you would like to learn more about what you were actually participating in feel free to read the sections below.

Thank you for playing with us.

- The Sevenfold Team

The Reason

What the community saw as "Sevenfold" was actually the testing ground for something much largerâ€”a comprehensive esports infrastructure platform I was developing to revolutionize how niche competitive gaming communities operate and grow. The concept originated during the WCI Finals viewing, developed in collaboration with an industry veteran who had been instrumental in funding and strategizing for major esports platforms including Faceit, Battlefy, Liquipedia, and start.gg. My vision was ambitious: create the first true all-in-one gaming and esports hub specifically targeting smaller, underserved competitive titles where organized competition was viable but infrastructure was lacking.

I chose World of Tanks as the proof of concept not because it was the end goal but because it represented the ideal testing environment... a niche title with passionate competitive players, existing community ties, and infrastructure gaps that needed solving. My plan was to perfect the model here before expanding to other underserved competitive gaming communities. My original collaborator, together with another prominent community figure I had contacted during development who expressed strong interest in the concept, invested heavily to support this vision. enterprise grade server hardware, premium database hosting with security and redundancy, professional branding, advanced project management tools, error monitoring systems, and premium API access... all paid months to years in advance to ensure stable development conditions.

The November 2025 launch timeline would have marked exactly one year since the WCI Finals sparked this initial concept, representing not just a product launch but the beginning of a new approach to the competitive gaming infrastructure development.

The Vision

When we started Sevenfold, our vision was ambitious: create the definitive platform for competitive World of Tanks pickup games. We dreamed of a system that would seamlessly match players of similar skill levels, provide comprehensive statistics, and foster a thriving competitive community.

We envisioned expanding beyond just matchmaking into a full ecosystem - tournament hosting, team formation tools, coaching platforms, and even integration with streaming services. The goal was to become the central hub where competitive World of Tanks players would gather, compete, and grow.

Our roadmap included features like AI-powered match analysis, automated replay reviews, personalized training recommendations, and partnerships with major World of Tanks content creators. We wanted to bridge the gap between casual play and professional esports, creating pathways for talented players to advance their skills and recognition.

While we achieved some of these goals and built meaningful connections within the community, the full vision remained just out of reach. The lessons learned and relationships formed during this journey, however, made every effort worthwhile.

Going Forward

While Sevenfold as a service has ended, the passion for competitive gaming and community building that drove this project continues. The team members are pursuing various paths - some continuing in game development, others exploring new technologies, and some taking well-deserved breaks.

For our community members looking for competitive World of Tanks experiences, we recommend exploring other platforms that have emerged in the ecosystem. The competitive scene continues to evolve, and new solutions are being developed by passionate developers who share our original vision.

We encourage everyone to stay connected with the friends and teammates you met through Sevenfold. The relationships formed through competitive gaming often outlast the platforms that brought us together. Consider joining Discord servers, forming your own teams, or participating in community-run tournaments.

Finally, to aspiring developers and entrepreneurs in the gaming space: don't let our closure discourage you. Building communities and platforms is challenging but incredibly rewarding work. Learn from our experiences, both positive and negative, and continue pushing the boundaries of what's possible in competitive gaming.

The gaming community needs passionate individuals willing to take risks and build something new. Whether it's in World of Tanks or any other competitive game, there are always opportunities to create meaningful experiences for players.

Community Impact

Throughout Sevenfold's operation, we facilitated thousands of competitive matches, bringing together players from across the globe. The friendships formed, skills developed, and memorable moments created represent the true success of our platform.

Many players used Sevenfold as a stepping stone to join established clans, participate in league play, or even advance to professional World of Tanks competitions. Seeing community members grow and succeed in the broader competitive scene was one of our greatest rewards.

The feedback, suggestions, and passionate engagement from our community shaped every aspect of Sevenfold's development. From feature requests to bug reports, tournament organization to content creation, the community was the heart of everything we built.

We hope the competitive spirit, sportsmanship, and camaraderie fostered through Sevenfold continues in whatever platforms and communities you join next. The skills, friendships, and experiences gained here are yours to keep forever.
